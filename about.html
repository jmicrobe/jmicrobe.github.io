I'm currently a graduate student in Bioinformatics at the University of Oregon. My research interests are centered around applying bioinformatic tools to analyze microbial data from next-generation sequencing output. I received my B.S. in Micro and Molecular Biology after working for two years in an extremophile microbial ecology lab. During that time I completed an independent research thesis supported by competitive grants from both the Oregon NASA Consortium and the Ronald E. McNair Scholars Program. I also had the great opportunity of joining my lab for a month at sea, collecting deep-sea hydrothermal vent samples near Tonga in the Pacific Ocean. My work in this lab ignited my passion for extreme environments, and astrobiology in particular.
